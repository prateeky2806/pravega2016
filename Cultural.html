Literary Decathlon

The Literary Decathlon is an amalgamation of various literary posers, that will test the literary and vocabulary skills, as well as the general awareness of the participants. This is a pioneering event that has been designed to suit the diversity among the participants as well as the audience. The name is derived for its having ten rounds, including a special qualifier round, which is called the Mixed Bag. This assortment involves some of the most classic puzzles ever posed in literary sphere. Progressive rounds test the speaking skills of the participants as well as their ability to display quick wit to a given situation.

Rules

Stage 1-Online Screening: Bands across the country send in their compositions online.Top 50 Bands (10 from each city) shall be selected for prelims in the respective 5 cities.

Stage 2- Prelims and Online Poll: The Top 50 Bands shall be judged by their performance at the prelims and by online poll on www.muslate.com. Prelims shall carry the 70% weightage ,while the online poll shall have the 30% share in the judgement.

Stage 3-Competitive Night: Top 5 Bands from all across the country shall battle it out at the final competitive night in Bangalore on 27th January.Bands shall be judged by eminent musicians of the country.This is the last stage which shall involve any type of judgement.Results won't be announced yet.

Stage 4-Gala Night: Top 5 Bands shall perform at the 1st pronite of Pravega'16-BoB 'Gala Night' without being judged for their performance. Gala night shall be headlined by a prominent Indian Band.The top 5 Bands of the Competitive night shall be announced.at the end of the event.

Rules

Prelims:
A selected number of teams will be called to perform in the finals. Participants will have to make a video highlighting their choreography and submit the link in the registration form.
1. This is a team event with a minimum of 6 members and a maximum of 12 members allowed per team. Only students with a valid college ID can take part.
2. Any number of teams from one college can participate. No participant can be a member of more than one team. Also, all members of a team need not necessarily be from the same college.
3.The time length of the submitted video must not exceed 4 minutes. The resolution of the video must be 480p or higher. All movements should be distinctly visible.
4. Videos of past performances are acceptable.
5. Costumes are not compulsory for the video prelims.
6. Upload your video to YouTube and email the link to lasya@pravega.org. Do keep in mind that the videos sent may be used for promotional purposes.
The last date for sending in your videos is 27th January 2016.

Finals
1. Teams registered must submit their tracks (finals - 10 minutes) and description by 28th January 2016 and are requested to bring a backup copy.
2. The following particulars are to be written in the mail: Name of the team leader and his/her phone number.
3. On the day of the event, team leaders must pick out spots at the specified time to decide the order of on-stage appearances.
4. In addition to the maximum permissible number of dancers, each team can have a maximum of 3 non-dancer members for music, lights and narration.
5. The performance must be between 4 and 10 minutes.
6. No additional time will be given for setting up the stage or props. Teams should clear the stage for the next team by themselves. (Props include objects carried by participants, such as sticks, swords, pots etc. Chairs, tables, screens and backdrops come under stage settings.)
7. Variety in songs, costumes and proper utilization of properties yields extra marks.
8. Vulgarity in any form is strictly prohibited. Use of water, fire or any inflammable substance is prohibited. Violation of rules will lead to disqualification.
9. The decision of the judges is final and no questions will be entertained in this regard.

Judgement criteria:
(For both Prelims & Finals)
1. Creativity and innovation.
2. Choreography and coordination.
3. Costumes, Song Selection and Properties.
4. Stage presence and style.

Rules

1. This is a team event with 2-15 members per team. This is open to schools, colleges, and theatre groups.
2. Members of a team need not necessarily be from the same college.
3. Members of a team need not necessarily be from the same college.

Finals:
1. Qualifying Teams must perform a play, highlighting a social issue that concerned India, at some point of time in our history.
2. Teams will be given a topic to perform, on the day of the finals.
3. Teams shall have to make a poster highlighting their message, which must be shown to the judges before the play.
4. No costumes and props will be provided.
5. The performance shouldn't be more than 20 minutes, in total. (Including Setup). Exceeding time limit may result in disqualification. (Judges’ discretion)
6. The contestants are strictly advised not to disclose the name of their group/institute to the Jury members/audience while introducing themselves on the stage.
7. A maximum of five props are allowed. Face painting, costumes and usage of masks are not considered props.
8. Use of mics, make-up and music systems is strictly NOT allowed. Teams can sing/dance by themselves to create the necessary effect. Playing unplugged musical instruments is allowed.
9. Theme / plot / dialogue of the play shall not implicitly or explicitly hurt any religious / nationalistic / patriotic feelings.
10. Teams shall be allowed to use only English and/or Hindi as the language of the play. Any deviation from this rule, can result in disqualification.
11. Use of vulgar language is prohibited. Points may be deducted for the same based on Judge’s discretion.
12. The Organising Committee reserves the right to change the venue, time and rules.
13. Decision of the judges will be final and binding.

Judgement Criteria:
1. Creativity.
2. Relevance.
3. Clarity of message.
4. Teamwork.
5. Involvement of audience
6. Poster

The quaint tribe of the biologists, and their legendary quest for refutation of the fact that they seem to have appeared out of nowhere about 10,000 years ago, have always been of immense interest to anthropologists worldwide. Unable to reconcile themselves with the fact that they alone, of all the peoples of this world, cannot claim to have had big, hairy ancestors who walked around on their knuckles, they compulsively erect 500 foot statues of gorillas in all their cities. Pravega doffs its hat to these natives. Watch out for librarians in gorilla suits though….

WHODUNNIT

When they first found conclusive evidence that they had, 10,000 years ago, appeared out of nowhere, the principal investigators who had been a part of this discovery were summarily lynched. After this, multiple forensic groups were formed and sent out in the hope that this piece of evidence would be found flawed. Whodunit is Pravega’s homage to this legendary, if ultimately unsuccessful, detective job.

INSTINCTIVE DIVE

With the realization that most of the evidence out there supports facts that are largely unpalatable, the tribes here have become much more amenable to theories that spring from nothing but intuition, and gut feeling. By honoring these ideas, they believe, they grow closer to that most revered of animals, the chimpanzee. Pravega’s very own Intuitive Dive is an event that draws its inspiration from these beliefs.

Rules:

1)Participants should come in teams of three.
2)The event consists of three rounds.
3)Preliminary round would consist of a question paper with 15-20 logical cases. (check suggestion)

6)In the forensic round, the teams would be provided with A general description of the crime scene
     a)A list of suspects and their history with the victim(s)
     b)A general description of the victim. ( i.e. habits, medical history and so forth)
     c)An autopsy report without any actual conclusions
     d) Other miscellaneous evidence and witness reports as and when asked for.
             i) The objective of the forensic round is to solve the case and find out the perpetrator of the crime.
            ii) The objective of the final round is to present the case with all the evidences and conclusions to judges.
7)IISc being an institute of prestige honours fair play and advocates the same. Any team suspected to be using any kind of unfair means during any of the rounds is liable for expulsion from the said event.
8)In all matters, the decision of the organizing committee, in consultation with the judges, is final and binding.
9)As a means of advocating fair play, the organizing committee does not allow any member of the IISc UG community to take part in the event. However, as IISc consists of a multitude of peoples, we do allow graduate as well as doctoral students of IISc to take part in the competition.
10)No undergraduate student of IISc is allowed to participate in the event, irrespective of whether the entire team consists of undergraduates, or a mix of graduate and doctoral students with undergraduates in the team.

Sintaya (meaning “Who knows?” in Elvish language from Lord of the Rings trilogy) is an annual theme based interdisciplinary science and technology quiz conducted at Pravega, IISc Bengaluru.

Structure:

The event consists of two rounds.
(i)    Prelims: In this rounds, participants from Bangalore are to write a pen and paper on Jan 24, 2016 exam with both objective and subjective questions (based on Science, Mathematics and technical subjects). Top four teams all India are selected for the next round.
(ii)    Quiz: The quiz will be conducted at IISc on 29th, 30th and 31st January, 2016. It will be a theme-based interdisciplinary science and technology quiz.

Prizes:

The total prize money for all four teams together will be ₹ 60,000.00.

Rules for the participants:
      For prelims:

      (i) Each team can have maximum of five members each.
      (ii) The entry fee for each team is ₹ 300.
      (iii) Teams can register online or at the centres one day before the prelims.
      (iv) The teams are to bring their own writing material.
      (v) The question paper will be in five separate sections.

      For quiz:

      (i) The theme for the quiz will be revealed only during the quiz. However,       the questions are concept based and facts necessary will be provided.

Rules:

Prelims:
1)Pen and paper round.
2)Answer 25 short questions, 2 marks each, in 1 hour.
3)Top 5 scorers selected.
4)Submission time written in each answer-sheet to resolve ties.
5)Logic and reasoning based questions that require only a basic knowledge of biology

Finals
Round One:
1) There exists one answer.
2) 10 hints given to reach the answer. Each hint a bit more directive towards the answer than the previous one.
3)The lower the number of hints taken to answer, the better.
4)After each hint, each team either chooses to answer, or chooses not to.
5)All of them or neither of them can choose to answer.
6)A team answers by writing the answer on the chits provided to them and submitting them. Each clue is projected for only one minute. Within this duration, any team that wants to check their answer must do so with the volunteer(s) on stage.
7)Points will depend upon the hint number. Correct answer will be awarded “220 - 20n” positive score and a wrong answer will fetch “110 - 10n” negative score, where n = 1, 2, 3… 9, 10.
8)A correct answer makes us move on to the second round, while a wrong answer just deducts the points of the participant and we move on to the next hint.
9)In case more than one team reaches the answer in the same hint, all of them will be given the assigned point for that hint.
10)Three of such questions will be there.

Round Two
1)A paragraph will be projected. It will contain the description of a certain biological occurrence.(//Contains a large body of facts and data related to the phenomenon. Hypothesis constructed based only on what is provided[either directly or indirectly]. Also, certain pieces of information provided can be ignored, but if this omission is justified; i.e., why it is not a major contributor to the phenomenon, brownie points! )
2)Each team will write down a reason for why they think this happens, and submit their answer.
3)They will get 5 minutes to answer.
4)Once the answer is submitted, it cannot be changed.
5)Then, a representative will come up on stage and read out their answer.
6)The judges will mark the answer on 200.
7)The answers need not be correct, but needs to be convincing.
The highest scorer wins. Score is independent of the score in the prelims.

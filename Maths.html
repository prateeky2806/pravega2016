PRAVEGA 2016 | Indian Institute of Science
Rules:
1)This event will have two rounds, a preliminary written round, followed by the final round.
2)The final round will comprise of several programming problems, most of which will only be unlocked after you've solved the earlier problems.
3)Once you've solved a problem, you also have the option of putting up the solution for sale. That means some other team, which has been unable to solve the problem, can choose to buy the solution of the problem from you, in exchange for a fraction of their points, which to you.
4)At the end of three hours, the team which has the maximum points and the team which has solved the maximum number of problems win.
5)The languages one can program their solutions in are the following: Python, C, C++ or Java.

Sintaya (meaning “Who knows?” in Elvish language from Lord of the Rings trilogy) is an annual theme based interdisciplinary science and technology quiz conducted at Pravega, IISc Bengaluru.

Structure:

The event consists of two rounds.
(i)    Prelims: In this rounds, participants from Bangalore are to write a pen and paper on Jan 24, 2016 exam with both objective and subjective questions (based on Science, Mathematics and technical subjects). Top four teams all India are selected for the next round.
(ii)    Quiz: The quiz will be conducted at IISc on 29th, 30th and 31st January, 2016. It will be a theme-based interdisciplinary science and technology quiz.

Prizes:

The total prize money for all four teams together will be ₹ 40,000.00.

Rules for the participants:
      For prelims:

      (i) Each team can have maximum of five members each.
      (ii) The entry fee for each team is ₹ 300.
      (iii) Teams can register online or at the centres one day before the prelims.
      (iv) The teams are to bring their own writing material.
      (v) The question paper will be in five separate sections.

      For quiz:

      (i) The theme for the quiz will be revealed only during the quiz. However,       the questions are concept based and facts necessary will be provided.

Rules:
1)Contestants are given some amount of money at the beginning of the game. They will not receive any money after this.
2)The game is split into rounds. In each round a fixed number of items are auctioned.
    a)The contestants will write down their bids. The bid values are not disclosed to other participants.
    b)The highest bidder will win the item being auctioned, but will have to pay the second highest bidders price.
    c)If someone is forced to pay more money than they currently possess, they are out of the game.
3)The objective of the game is to maximize one’s total value. The value a contestant has is the sum of the value of the money he/she possesses plus that of the items he has.
4)At the instant an item is bought it has a certain fixed value. This price does not change from item to item.
5)At the end of each round, the value of the money a contestant has decreases by 10%. The initial value of some amount of money is the same as the amount.
6)At the end of each round, the value of a contestant’s goods increases by 10%.
7)At the end of all the rounds , the contestant with the highest value wins.
